My favorite book of all time would be The Wretched of the Earth by Frantz Fanon, as it incisively examines the psychological and cultural effects of colonialism on African identity. Through passionate analysis and powerful insights, Fanon explores the struggles for liberation and the reclaiming of self. This profound work has deepened my understanding of my own history and the ongoing quest for justice in Africa.

My favorite hiking spot is Mount Zebidar, which I visited during the Meskel Festival while staying with my grandmother. I hiked through the forests and took in the stunning views. The experience of being in such a beautiful place with family made it truly special.

Korean movies have become a fun hobby of mine, as they always bring a mix of excitement and heartfelt storytelling. I love getting lost in the vibrant characters and unique plots that each film offers. It's a fantastic way to unwind and explore a different culture.
